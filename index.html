The Two Finalists

It's come down to these

Both toured, both loved. One is a grand historic hotel in the heart of downtown; the other is a restored 1900s church under a projection-mapped dome. Two very different nights.

Finalist

The Josephine

Uptown · Napoleon Ave

A restored early-1900s church reimagined by Dickie Brennan & Co. — a 5,200 sq ft former sanctuary with a soaring dome and open-air courtyards under the oaks. The most photogenic room of anywhere we've seen.

  • Projection-mapped dome over the dance floor — a true "wow" moment
  • Ceremony outdoors in the string-lit South Courtyard
  • The Grotto (stonework + neon) for photos; a bridal suite above the hall
  • In-house catering, event team & security
~$40k venue + food + bar for ~200 (Fri, Apr 2 2027 proposal in hand). Roughly $120–130 per guest all-in.
Finalist

NOPSI Hotel

Central Business District

A grand historic hotel downtown with in-house everything and rooftop views. Its superpower: guests are already home — they ride an elevator, not a rideshare, at the end of the night.

  • Everyone stays on-site — walk-home convenience
  • Second line flows naturally right out into the CBD
  • Hotel provides tables, dance floor, stage & lighting
  • Our contact offered to "match just about anything"
$145–$225 / guest reception packages by tier (the richer tiers add hot stations + a late-night snack). Chairs to be swapped — Amanda's call.
A Night at The Josephine

How the evening would flow

Picture the whole night, start to finish — the piece that turns a beautiful room into an easy, effortless celebration.

Aft.

Gather at the hotel

Out-of-town guests are all blocked in one downtown hotel — a shared basecamp. Meet at the lobby bar, then a chartered shuttle carries everyone to the door. No one hunts for a ride.

6:30

Arrival & the ceremony drink

Guests step off the shuttle onto oak-lined Napoleon Ave into the courtyard. A cocktail is waiting — served from a rented center bar wrapped in a floral "tree," the visual centerpiece of the entrance.

7:00

Ceremony under the towers

Outdoors in the South Courtyard, golden-hour light, ceremony musicians. Intimate and unmistakably New Orleans.

7:30

Cocktail hour in the courtyards

Guests drift between the courtyards and the Grotto (the photo moment) while the main hall is set. Passed bites, the center bar keeps pouring.

8:00

The reveal — the Main Hall

Doors open to the former sanctuary. The dome lights up with projection mapping, the band takes the stage, and the dance floor is front and center. Stations and grazing all night — a party, not a sit-down.

Late

Second line & the ride home

A brass-led second line loops the courtyards for the send-off energy, glow handouts for the finish — then the shuttle loops everyone back to the hotel bar for the after-party.
