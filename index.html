The Two Finalists

It's come down to these

Both toured, both loved. One is a grand historic hotel in the heart of downtown; the other is a restored 1900s church under a projection-mapped dome. Two very different nights.

Finalist

The Josephine

Uptown · Napoleon Ave

A restored early-1900s church reimagined by Dickie Brennan & Co. — a 5,200 sq ft former sanctuary with a soaring dome and open-air courtyards under the oaks. The most photogenic room of anywhere we've seen.

  • Projection-mapped dome over the dance floor — a true "wow" moment
  • Ceremony outdoors in the string-lit South Courtyard
  • The Grotto (stonework + neon) for photos; a bridal suite above the hall
  • In-house catering, event team & security
~$37–40k venue + food + bar for ~200. Official proposal in hand: $39,775 build (Fri, Apr 2 2027), 23% service + 10.75% F&B tax, $7k rental / $10k F&B min. Roughly $185–200/guest on venue+F&B.
Finalist

NOPSI Hotel

Central Business District

A grand historic hotel downtown with in-house everything and rooftop views. Its superpower: guests are already home — they ride an elevator, not a rideshare, at the end of the night.

  • Everyone stays on-site — walk-home convenience (216 rooms)
  • Second line flows naturally right out into the CBD
  • Hotel provides tables, dance floor, stage & lighting (lighting hinted free)
  • Tara (our contact) offered to "match just about anything"
~$48k base proposal (220) Actual quote on the table: $48,168 for 220, tax incl., 3-hr open bar — the lightest food tier. Stepping up to Krauss ($182/pp) → ~$62k or Maison Blanche ($225/pp) → ~$74k is the real swing. Amanda's chair swap on top.
The negotiation lever

NOPSI can very plausibly land near Josephine's number

NOPSI's base proposal already lands it within ~$8–12k of Josephine, not the $22–34k above that the first model assumed. And on top of that, Tara said she'd match nearly anything.

The play: pick the package tier we actually want (likely Krauss, for "nobody leaves hungry"), get Josephine's number in writing, then ask Tara to match it. If she does, NOPSI drops toward ~$117k all-in while keeping the hotel/convenience edge.

A Night at The Josephine

How the evening would flow

The Josephine's one open question is that it isn't attached to a hotel. The fix: block one anchor hotel downtown and run a shuttle loop — so guests gather at a bar, ride to a stunning church, and ride home. Here's the whole night.

Aft.

Gather at the hotel

Out-of-town guests are all blocked in one downtown hotel — a shared basecamp. Meet at the lobby bar, then a chartered shuttle carries everyone to the door. No one hunts for a ride. Amanda preps in the Josephine Suite (confirming availability).

6:30

Arrival & the ceremony drink

Guests step off the shuttle onto oak-lined Napoleon Ave into the South Courtyard. A cocktail is waiting — served from a rented center bar wrapped in a floral "tree," the visual centerpiece of the entrance.

7:00

Ceremony under the towers

Outdoors in the South Courtyard, golden-hour light, ceremony musicians. Intimate and unmistakably New Orleans.

7:30

Cocktail hour in the courtyards

Guests drift between the courtyards and the Grotto (the photo moment) while the main hall is set. Passed bites, the center bar keeps pouring.

8:00

The reveal — the Main Hall

Doors open to the former sanctuary. The dome lights up with projection mapping, the band takes the stage, and the dance floor is front and center. Stations and grazing all night — a party, not a sit-down (only ~30–40% seated).

Late

Second line & the ride home

A brass-led second line — from Uptown it's a contained loop around the courtyards/block rather than a neighborhood parade — glow handouts for the finish, then the shuttle loops everyone back to the hotel bar for the after-party.
